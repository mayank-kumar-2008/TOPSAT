PERSISTENT HOMOLOGY

Known terrain forms a manifold. Novel terrain creates holes.

  • The archive of known terrain occupies compact clusters on the hypersphere.
  • Ripser filtration detects H₁ structural holes in the manifold — mathematically guaranteed.
  • Novel terrain cannot be assigned to any cluster. It stands alone.
BOTTLENECK DISTANCE

Every tile gets a novelty score.

  • Bottleneck distance between tile H₁ diagram and archive class diagrams.
  • High distance = structurally unprecedented. Transmit first.
  • Low distance = seen before. Skip or defer.
